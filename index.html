About

I am a Postdoctoral Associate at Yale University, advised by Fan Li. My research focuses on developing causal inference and survival analysis methods for modern biomedical studies, with particular emphasis on composite endpoints and cluster-randomized and stepped-wedge designs. I am also interested in principled approaches for borrowing historical information and in integrating machine learning tools into study design and analysis. I earned my Ph.D. in Biostatistics from Medical College of Wisconsin.
